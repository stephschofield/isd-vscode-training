# Meridian Innovation Summit — Program Recommendation

## TL;DR

We recommend a one-day, three-track Innovation Summit. The tracks
anchor the three themes that drew the strongest signal from speakers
and stakeholders: Platform Engineering Pragmatism, Production AI, and
Reliability and Security as Everyday Practice. We need your approval
and three named curators within two weeks to lock the program by
quarter end.

## Recommendation

We recommend running the inaugural Innovation Summit as a single day with
three parallel tracks, each anchored to one of the three highest-signal
themes from our submission and stakeholder synthesis: **Platform
Engineering Pragmatism**, **Production AI that Survives Contact with
Reality**, and **Reliability and Security as Everyday Practice**. A
30-minute lightning-talk block in the late afternoon will surface the
strongest Developer Experience and Engineering Leadership content
without forcing a fourth track. We are asking you to approve this
program shape so the curation team can lock the final session list within
two weeks.

## Why this program shape

Submissions and stakeholders point the same way. Two-thirds of the 30
cleaned submissions cluster into three themes — platform engineering,
production AI, and continuous reliability and security work — and
stakeholder interviews named the same three. Our audience survey
reinforced it: the demand is for field-tested patterns, not theory.
Building tracks around those themes is the lowest-risk path to a
coherent Year 1 in the time we have left.

This shape also serves how attendees actually wayfind. Three
theme-locked tracks let an attendee pick a track and stay. Mixing themes
inside tracks — which we considered — adds cognitive load without
surfacing more content than the lightning block already does.
Outcome-based tracks are interesting on paper but require re-tagging
every session, and the mapping is contestable enough that the curation
risk outweighs the upside this year.

What we'll learn by running this shape is which of the three themes
draws the strongest day-of attendance, and whether the lightning block
is the right home for cross-cutting content. Both inform Year 2.

## Themes (program tracks)

**Platform engineering pragmatism (Track A)** — field-tested patterns
for shipping internal platforms in realistic timelines. Serves senior
engineers and engineering leaders tired of theoretical platform talks.

**Production AI that survives contact with reality (Track B)** —
operational practices that keep AI features honest after launch:
evaluation, monitoring, drift, cost. Serves the practitioners who own
AI features in production.

**Reliability and security as everyday practice (Track C)** — small,
continuous reliability and security work embedded in product teams
rather than owned by a specialist team. Serves SREs and security
engineers who want lightweight rituals their teams will actually adopt.

**Developer experience + Engineering leadership (lightning block)** —
the strongest sessions on onboarding, internal APIs, refactoring, and
leadership in non-hypergrowth orgs. Surfaces in the late-afternoon
lightning block.

## Risks and tradeoffs

- **We're under-serving Developer Experience and Engineering Leadership
  content.** Both have strong submissions. The lightning block partially
  covers it; track-hoppers fully cover it. We accept that some attendees
  will want a fourth track.
- **Cross-theme sessions don't have a natural home.** We'll surface them
  in the strongest matching track and accept the imperfect fit rather
  than diluting wayfinding.
- **One day means hard choices on session count.** With three tracks
  × five slots, we have 15 session slots and ~30 strong submissions.
  That curation pressure is the main reason we need named curators in
  two weeks, not four.
- **Running outcome-based tracks would be more interesting but
  significantly riskier this year.** We're flagging it as a Year 2
  candidate if Year 1 lands.
