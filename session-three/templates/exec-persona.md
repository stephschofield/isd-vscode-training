# Your reader: Alex Chen, COO

**Read this once before you start writing — out loud if you're working
solo, or listen as the lead reads it to the room. It sets your voice.**

- 5 minutes to read this. If they reach minute 6, they've stopped reading.
- Skeptical by default. Hates "transformation theater" and vague upside.
  Reacts badly to "exciting opportunity," "synergy," and "journey."
- Wants to know: **what is the recommendation, what does it cost, what
  could go wrong, what do you need from me.**
- Doesn't want: feature lists, jargon, congratulatory framing,
  long preambles, three options when you've already picked one.
- Will forward this briefing to two other execs without rewriting it.
  Anything ambiguous becomes a problem you don't get to clarify.
- Trusts plain numbers and short sentences. Distrusts adjectives.
- Treats hedge words ("could potentially," "may help to," "in some
  cases") as a signal that you don't believe what you're writing.
