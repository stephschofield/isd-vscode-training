# Themes — Lab 2 output

These are the five themes that emerged from synthesizing the cleaned
speaker submissions, stakeholder interviews, and audience-survey signals
during Lab 2. Each theme is the evidence pool for one program track.

> **How to read this file:** each section is a theme with a one-paragraph
> definition, the signals that produced it, and the rough volume of
> matching submissions. Use these as the spine when writing the Themes
> section of your briefing — the one-paragraph definition is the model
> for what you should write.

---

## Theme 1 — Platform engineering pragmatism

Engineering organizations are tired of theoretical platform conversations
and want field-tested patterns for shipping internal platforms in
realistic timelines (weeks to a couple of quarters, not multi-year
programs). This theme surfaced strongly in both submissions and
stakeholder interviews — the appetite is for talks that name what they
tried, what broke, and what they kept.

- **Signal strength:** very high (8 submissions, 6 stakeholder mentions)
- **Sample sessions:** S001, S003, S006, S009, S012, S018, S021, S025

## Theme 2 — Production AI that survives contact with reality

Submissions clustered around the gap between AI demos and AI in
production: evaluation, monitoring, drift, cost, and the operational
practices that keep AI features honest after launch. Stakeholders
asked specifically for talks that aren't framed as "look at the cool
demo" but as "here's what it took to keep it working at month six."

- **Signal strength:** very high (6 submissions, 5 stakeholder mentions)
- **Sample sessions:** S005, S010, S011, S019, S027, S030

## Theme 3 — Reliability and security as everyday practice

Reliability and security work that is small, continuous, and embedded
in product teams' day-to-day, rather than centralized in a separate
specialist team. Stakeholders explicitly named this as the gap they want
the summit to fill — they have plenty of conference content on
heavyweight security programs, and zero on lightweight rituals.

- **Signal strength:** high (7 submissions, 4 stakeholder mentions)
- **Sample sessions:** S004, S008, S014, S015, S022, S023, S028

## Theme 4 — Developer experience that compounds

Investments in developer experience — onboarding, internal APIs,
tooling, refactoring legacy code — framed by the second-order effects
they produce (faster product velocity, lower attrition) rather than as
ends in themselves. This theme was the late-emerging pattern in
synthesis — multiple submissions that looked unrelated turned out to
share this throughline.

- **Signal strength:** medium-high (5 submissions, 3 stakeholder mentions)
- **Sample sessions:** S007, S016, S020, S025, S029

## Theme 5 — Engineering leadership in non-hypergrowth orgs

Leadership content for engineering managers and directors operating in
companies that aren't hypergrowth tech-first companies — including
non-tech-first orgs, post-hypergrowth orgs, and small focused teams.
Stakeholders called out that most leadership content assumes a context
their leaders don't actually live in.

- **Signal strength:** medium (3 submissions, 4 stakeholder mentions)
- **Sample sessions:** S013, S017, S026
