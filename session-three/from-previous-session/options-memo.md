# Options memo — Meridian Innovation Summit program shape

> **Lab 2 output.** This memo names three program-shape options and
> recommends one. In Lab 3 you are advocating for the recommended
> option, not re-deciding among the three.

## Background

We have ~30 cleaned speaker submissions ([`clean_submissions.csv`](clean_submissions.csv))
clustered into 5 themes ([`themes.md`](themes.md)). Stakeholder interviews
confirmed that audiences want field-tested patterns over theoretical
content, and that two themes — **Platform engineering pragmatism** and
**Production AI that survives contact with reality** — carry the most
audience pull. The summit has one day of program time and three
parallel tracks (room capacity).

## Options

### Option A — Tracks-by-theme, one day, three tracks

Map each track to one theme. Run the three highest-signal themes:
Platform engineering pragmatism, Production AI that survives contact
with reality, and Reliability and security as everyday practice. The
remaining two themes (Developer experience, Engineering leadership)
become a small lightning-talk block.

- **Pros:** Clean wayfinding. Attendees pick a track and stay. Each
  track has 6+ candidate sessions, so we can cherry-pick.
- **Cons:** Forces hard either/or choices for attendees with overlapping
  interests. Themes 4 and 5 get under-served.

### Option B — Two tracks-by-theme + one floating "best of" track

Run two theme-locked tracks (Platform, Production AI) and a third
"best of the rest" track that mixes the strongest sessions from
Reliability, Developer experience, and Engineering leadership.

- **Pros:** Surfaces strong sessions that don't fit a single-theme
  track. More flexible for content that crosses themes.
- **Cons:** The mixed track is harder to wayfinding-tag in the program
  and risks feeling like leftovers. Cognitive load for attendees.

### Option C — Outcome-based tracks (not theme-based)

Re-cut tracks around audience outcomes: "Ship faster," "Sleep at night,"
"Lead better." Each outcome track pulls from multiple themes.

- **Pros:** Closer to how attendees describe their problems. Highest
  audience-language alignment.
- **Cons:** Requires re-tagging every session. Curation is significantly
  more work and is the riskiest path schedule-wise. The mapping from
  themes to outcomes is contestable.

## Recommendation

**Go with Option A.** Three tracks — **Platform engineering pragmatism**,
**Production AI that survives contact with reality**, and **Reliability
and security as everyday practice** — with a 30-minute lightning-talk
block in the late afternoon for the strongest Developer-experience and
Engineering-leadership sessions.

### Why

- Themes are the strongest audience signal we have. Stakeholders, the
  submissions volume, and survey signals all point the same direction.
  Don't dilute that.
- Option A is the lowest-risk path to a coherent program in the time
  remaining. Curation is straightforward. Wayfinding is obvious.
- The lightning block preserves the strongest Theme-4 and Theme-5
  content without forcing a fourth track.
- Option C is the most interesting on paper but the highest-risk in
  practice; reserve it for next year if Year 1 lands.

### What we'd be giving up

- Cross-theme sessions don't get a natural home — we'd surface them in
  the strongest matching track and accept the imperfect fit.
- Some attendees with split interests will track-hop, which is fine.

### What we need to decide

1. **Approve Option A** as the program shape.
2. **Assign a curator per track** to lock the final session list within
   2 weeks.
3. **Confirm the lightning-block format** (5 talks × 6 minutes vs. 3 talks
   × 10 minutes) — recommend the latter for substance.
