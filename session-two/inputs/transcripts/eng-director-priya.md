# Stakeholder interview — Priya Raman

- **Speaker:** Priya Raman, Senior Engineering Director
- **Role at Meridian:** Representing engineering leadership at the Summit
- **Interviewer:** Steph Schofield (program team)
- **Date:** March 25, 2026
- **Format:** 30-minute video call, transcribed
- **Note:** Speaker turns only. Light cleanup for readability. Priya
  asked us to flag that she was deliberately blunt — "you asked for the
  engineering perspective; I am giving it to you."

---

**Priya:** Thank you for asking me. I'll be direct because that is more
useful to you than being polite. I think the Summit, in the shape it's
heading, is going to under-serve the people who actually build things
at Meridian. I want to flag that now while there's still time to fix it.

**Priya:** Here is what I keep saying and what I will say again on
record: developer experience is a strategic investment for this
company, not a comfort item. The reason our cloud bills are high is
partly that engineers have to wait six minutes for a CI run and then
ten more minutes to figure out which test failed and why. The reason
some of our customer-facing teams are slow to respond is partly that
their internal tools were built in a hurry three years ago and nobody
has been allowed to invest in them since. DX shows up in cloud cost,
in customer responsiveness, in retention of senior engineers, and in
how fast we ship. It is not, despite what Jane and Marcus apparently
believe, an inward-facing topic.

**Priya:** I have heard, more than once now, that DX would "bore the
audience." I disagree, and I think it reveals an assumption about who
is in the audience. The Summit audience is largely people who *use*
internal platforms every day. They will not be bored by a credible
discussion of why those platforms either help them or get in their way.
What would bore them is the version where we talk about DX in
abstract, framework-pitching terms. That's a presentation problem,
not a topic problem.

**Priya:** I will also say this directly because it needs to be said:
I do not think the Summit should be shaped to make Meridian look good
externally. That is a marketing program, not a Summit program. If we
optimize the agenda for press coverage we will end up with a program
where the most visible sessions are the least useful internally, and
the people in the room will feel patronized. I would rather skip the
brand angle entirely and have a Summit that genuinely earns word of
mouth over the next twelve months.

**Priya:** AI in workflows — I'm fine with that as a direction. The
submissions are good. The risk is we end up with thirty variations of
the same talk. You'll need to be aggressive about variety inside that
direction: real workflows, different roles, honest about what didn't
work, not just the wins. If every AI session is "and then we shipped
it and it was great," the audience will tune out by lunch.

**Priya:** Cloud spending — yes, important, but please make sure at
least one of the cloud sessions is honest about the part where we
haven't fully figured it out. The audience can smell a curated success
story from the back of the room.

**Priya:** Customer stories — fine, important, no notes.

**Priya:** Quantum computing came up in some kickoff conversation.
Please don't. We don't do quantum work. We have no expertise in
quantum work. Inviting an external person to do a quantum talk
because someone thought it would look good is exactly the kind of
"performative" thing Marcus warned about, and I will be slightly
hostile about it if it appears on the agenda.

**Priya:** On the tracks question. Jane wants three deep tracks.
Marcus wants six narrower ones. I think six. The submission base
supports it, and three tracks force us to lump things that don't
naturally lump. The "we honor the submissions" argument matters more
than the "fewer things to remember" argument, in my view, because the
audience is technical enough to handle parallel tracks.

**Priya:** I will be back in the room when you present the program
options. If DX is not one of the options, I'm going to ask why on
record. I would rather we have that conversation now in this
transcript than have it as a surprise in front of the broader
leadership team.
