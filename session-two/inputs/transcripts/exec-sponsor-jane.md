# Stakeholder interview — Jane Okonkwo

- **Speaker:** Jane Okonkwo, VP, Office of the CEO
- **Role at Meridian:** Executive sponsor for the Innovation Summit
- **Interviewer:** Steph Schofield (program team)
- **Date:** March 11, 2026
- **Format:** 30-minute video call, transcribed
- **Note:** Speaker turns only. Light cleanup for readability.

---

**Jane:** Thanks for setting this up. Look, the Summit this year has to
do something different. Last year was a competent corporate event. We
had good speakers, the room was full, the satisfaction scores were fine.
Fine is the problem. The board has noticed that our peers are getting
written about — Forrester pieces, podcast appearances, the kind of
inbound where a customer mentions a Northwind talk to one of our AEs and
we have to nod and pretend we saw it. I want this Summit to be the moment
people inside *and* outside Meridian say, "okay, those people are
serious."

**Jane:** The thing I keep coming back to is — we have something most of
our peers don't, which is a workforce that's been quietly using AI in
the day-to-day for a couple of years now. Not the moonshot stuff. The
boring middle: how a customer success manager triages her inbox now, how
the legal team drafts a first pass on a contract, how the planning team
spins up a forecast in twenty minutes instead of two days. That's the
story I want this Summit to tell. AI in real workflows, by the people
who actually do the work. Not a keynote about the future of AI. A room
full of people from finance and ops and account management saying, "this
is what changed for me last quarter."

**Jane:** Cloud cost. I know everyone's exhausted by it. But our CFO
asked me twice in the last month why we don't have a clearer story
externally about how we manage cloud spend. We've done good work on it.
We have specific people who could tell that story credibly. If we don't
include something on responsible cloud spending, we're missing a chance
to show financial discipline at a moment when the market wants to hear
about it.

**Jane:** Customer stories. Not the marketing kind. I want a real account
manager standing on stage describing what one specific customer asked
for, how the field team responded, what they got wrong, what they
learned. The Summit needs at least one of those per day or it'll feel
abstract. I keep saying "real stories from inside the company" — that
phrase has come up in three of my prep meetings now. Write that down.

**Jane:** What I would also love — and I know this is an "exec ask" so
push back if you have to — is that the Summit raises Meridian's
external profile. Press, podcasts, the recordings going somewhere people
can find them. Not in a desperate way. In the way where if a journalist
or analyst is curious about how a financial services platform actually
runs, they have something to point at. I will say this once and not be
defensive about it: I want the brand benefit. That's an honest part of
why I'm sponsoring this.

**Jane:** Tracks? I don't have a strong opinion on the structure. My
instinct is fewer, deeper tracks beats more, shorter ones — three big
themes the audience can actually follow versus six fragmented ones. But
if Marcus tells me operationally we need six because that's how the
submissions land, I'll defer. Just don't slice it so thin nobody
remembers what they came for.

**Jane:** The thing I want you to *not* do is let this become a research
showcase. We have very smart people doing very interesting things, and
some of those things are not relevant to anyone outside our walls. I'd
rather a Summit that an outside partner could attend and walk away with
something usable than a Summit that wins us internal applause. If you
have to make a tradeoff between depth and accessibility, choose
accessibility. We can do depth in our internal tech meetups.

**Jane:** Last thing. I trust you and the program team to figure out the
shape. But the Summit has to make Meridian look like a company that
takes its own platform seriously, that uses AI where it's useful, that
spends cloud money like adults, and that has real customers who can
speak for it. If we hit those four notes I'll consider this a win.
Anything that doesn't serve those notes — I'm comfortable cutting.
