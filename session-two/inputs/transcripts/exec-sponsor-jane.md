# Stakeholder interview — Jane Okonkwo

- **Speaker:** Jane Okonkwo, VP, Office of the CEO
- **Role at Meridian:** Executive sponsor for the Innovation Summit
- **Interviewer:** Steph Schofield (program team)
- **Date:** March 11, 2026
- **Format:** 30-minute video call, transcribed
- **Note:** Speaker turns only. Light cleanup for readability.

---

**Jane:** Thanks for setting this up. The Summit this year has to do
something different. Last year was competent corporate — good speakers,
full room, fine satisfaction scores. Fine is the problem. The board
has noticed peers getting written about — Forrester pieces, podcasts,
the kind of inbound where a customer mentions a Northwind talk to one
of our AEs and we have to nod and pretend we saw it. I want this Summit
to be the moment people inside *and* outside Meridian say, "okay,
those people are serious."

**Jane:** What I keep coming back to — we have something most peers
don't, a workforce quietly using AI day-to-day for years. Not the
moonshot stuff. The boring middle: how a customer success manager
triages her inbox now, how legal drafts a first pass on a contract, how
planning spins up a forecast in twenty minutes instead of two days.
That's the story I want this Summit to tell. AI in real workflows, by
the people who do the work. Not a keynote about the future of AI — a
room of finance, ops, and account management saying, "this is what
changed for me last quarter."

**Jane:** Cloud cost. Everyone's exhausted by it. But our CFO asked me
twice last month why we don't have a clearer external story about
cloud spend. We've done good work on it, with people who could tell
that story credibly. Skip it and we miss a chance to show financial
discipline at a moment when the market wants to hear it.

**Jane:** Customer stories. Not the marketing kind. I want a real account
manager on stage describing what one specific customer asked for, how
the field team responded, what they got wrong, what they learned. The
Summit needs at least one of those per day or it'll feel abstract. I
keep saying "real stories from inside the company" — that phrase has
come up in three of my prep meetings now.

**Jane:** Push back if you have to, but I'd also love the Summit to
raise Meridian's external profile. Press, podcasts, recordings people
can find. Not desperate — the kind where if a journalist or analyst is
curious how a financial services platform actually runs, they have
something to point at. I'll say this once and not be defensive: I want
the brand benefit. That's an honest part of why I'm sponsoring this.

**Jane:** Tracks? No strong opinion. My instinct is fewer, deeper
beats more, shorter — three big themes the audience can follow versus
six fragmented ones. But if Marcus says we operationally need six
because that's how the submissions land, I'll defer. Just don't slice
it so thin nobody remembers what they came for.

**Jane:** What I want you to *not* do is let this become a research
showcase. We have very smart people doing very interesting things, some
of which aren't relevant to anyone outside our walls. I'd rather a
Summit an outside partner could attend and walk away with something
usable than one that wins us internal applause. If you have to trade
depth for accessibility, choose accessibility. Depth lives in our
internal tech meetups.

**Jane:** Last thing. I trust you and the program team to figure out the
shape. But the Summit has to make Meridian look like a company that
takes its own platform seriously, uses AI where it's useful, spends
cloud money like adults, and has real customers who can speak for it.
Hit those four notes and I'll consider this a win. Anything that
doesn't serve them — I'm comfortable cutting.
