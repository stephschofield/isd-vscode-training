# Stakeholder interview — Marcus Hale

- **Speaker:** Marcus Hale, Director, Program Management Office
- **Role at Meridian:** Operational owner of the Innovation Summit
- **Interviewer:** Steph Schofield (program team)
- **Date:** March 18, 2026
- **Format:** 45-minute video call, transcribed
- **Note:** Speaker turns only. Light cleanup for readability.

---

**Marcus:** Okay, so I'm going to be the operational guy in this
conversation, which means I'm going to be a little less inspirational
than Jane was. That's fine, somebody has to. The thing I'm watching for
is whether the Summit lands as a *coherent program* or as a string of
talks that happen to be on the same week. The audience is going to
remember a structure long after they forget any individual session.

**Marcus:** Submission volume is up. We've got just under two hundred
submissions in the cleaned dataset, which is good — it means we have
selection pressure. The risk with that volume is we end up with a
program that feels like a buffet. So my first ask is: pick a small
number of program directions, put a name on each one, and then ruthlessly
sort submissions into them. Anything that doesn't fit a direction either
gets cut, or you create a new direction for it deliberately. Don't let
random good talks pull the program out of shape.

**Marcus:** AI in everyday workflows is real. I've got the data — we have
forty-eight submissions on it, which is more than any other category.
Most of them are good, several are excellent. I think you can build a
strong direction around that and have plenty of selection.

**Marcus:** Cloud spending — yeah, that's a real one too. We have a solid
batch of submissions on FinOps and cost discipline. It maps to what
Jane wants, which is helpful. The risk is the topic feels boring next to
AI, so we'll need to be careful about adjacency in the schedule.

**Marcus:** Customer stories. I want this to be a *full track*, not a
sprinkle. The submissions are there. The challenge is getting field
folks comfortable on stage. I'd rather we cap that direction at six or
seven sessions and make every one of them excellent than try to fill
ten and water it down.

**Marcus:** On the structure debate — Jane wants three big themes, I
think we should consider six smaller tracks. Here's why. With three
themes, attendees pick one and live in it for the day. With six tracks,
they get more variety, and we honor more of the submission base. We have
the rooms. We have the time slots. And operationally six tracks is
*easier* for me, not harder, because I don't have to force-fit talks
into themes that don't quite want them. I think we should put both
options in front of leadership and let them choose, but I'm going to
recommend six.

**Marcus:** I want to flag something Priya raised at the offsite that I
think is worth taking seriously. She's been pushing for developer
experience as its own program direction. Not just "tooling" — a real
strategic frame around DX as an investment that compounds. We have
about thirty submissions on it, which is enough to fill a track. I know
Jane is lukewarm on it because it sounds inward-facing. I think she's
half right. There's a version of that direction that *is* inward-facing
and would bore everyone. There's also a version that's about how we
ship faster and more reliably, which is exactly the kind of "we run our
own platform like adults" story Jane says she wants. If we frame it
right, it serves both of them. I'd push for including it.

**Marcus:** There's a topic in the kickoff notes I want to be honest
about: someone — I think it was Henrik — floated a session on quantum
computing. It came up twice. We do not have a single submission on it.
We do not have an in-house speaker who could credibly do it. I would
not include it. If anyone pushes back, I'll show them the dataset.

**Marcus:** The external brand angle Jane keeps mentioning — I get it,
but I'd be careful. If we orient the Summit around brand outcomes, the
program will feel performative. Better to make a Summit that's actually
useful to the people in the room and let the brand benefit follow. If
we want a "for the cameras" element, we can record a few sessions and
distribute them after. We don't have to bend the program shape around
it.

**Marcus:** The thing I do *not* want to ship is a program where
attendees can't tell why a session was included. Every session should
trace to a stated direction, with stated criteria, that we wrote down
before we started picking. That's the operational ask. The strategy
shape is yours and Jane's call, but the discipline of mapping
submissions to directions — that one's mine, and I'm going to be
annoying about it.
