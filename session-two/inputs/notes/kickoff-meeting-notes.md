# Innovation Summit — Kickoff Meeting Notes

- **Date:** March 4, 2026
- **Attendees:** Jane Okonkwo, Marcus Hale, Priya Raman, Henrik Sørensen
  (CFO chief of staff), Steph Schofield, Gareth Mwangi (program team)
- **Note-taker:** Steph
- **Meeting length:** 60 minutes

---

- Jane opens. "This Summit needs to be the moment we stop being polite
  about what we're good at."
- Stated north star: a Summit that makes Meridian look like a serious
  platform company, internally and externally.
- Top themes Jane wants in the program:
  - AI in everyday workflows (not the moonshot)
  - Responsible cloud spending
  - Real customer stories from inside the company
  - "External brand visibility" — Jane wants the Summit to drive press
    and analyst attention. Acknowledges this is an exec ask.
- Marcus: ~200 cleaned submissions. Plenty to work with. Need program
  shape decisions before talk selection, not after.
- Priya: pushes for **developer experience** as a strategic direction in
  its own right. "It shows up in cloud cost, in customer responsiveness,
  in retention." Notes she will bring this back at every meeting until
  it's either accepted or explicitly rejected. (Steph: it's worth taking
  this seriously — the dataset has ~30 DX submissions.)
- Henrik suggests **quantum computing** as a "look-ahead" session.
  Frames it as "we should be where the puck is going." No specific
  speaker named, no internal expertise identified. Marcus flags he
  hasn't seen a single quantum submission. Henrik says we could invite
  an external speaker. Jane: "interesting, park it."
- Henrik returns to quantum twice more in the meeting — once when
  discussing "future-facing" content, once unprompted near the end.
  Steph notes the repetition.
- Discussion of program structure:
  - Jane: leans 3 deep tracks. "Audience can actually follow it."
  - Marcus: leans 6 narrower tracks. "Honors the submission base, easier
    operationally."
  - Priya: agrees with Marcus. 6 tracks.
  - Decision: program team will present both options in the strategic
    options memo with stated tradeoffs.
- Open question (parked): how do we handle the press / external content
  goal? Jane wants it. Priya pushes back hard. Marcus pragmatic ("we can
  record sessions, distribute after, don't need to bend the program
  shape"). Decision: defer; revisit once program directions are drafted.
- Action items:
  - Steph + Gareth: produce a draft program directions memo within two
    weeks.
  - Marcus: confirm room and slot availability for the 3-track and
    6-track scenarios.
  - Jane: send a one-paragraph framing message to the broader leadership
    team so the room knows what we're optimizing for.
  - Priya: will share a one-pager on the DX framing she has in mind.
- Closes 11:02. Energy in the room: cordial, with one real disagreement
  (DX framing) and one persistent ask that doesn't have data behind it
  (quantum).
