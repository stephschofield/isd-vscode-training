# Innovation Summit — Program Directions

## Context

The Meridian Innovation Summit needs a program shape, not just a talk
list. We have ~190 cleaned speaker submissions and three converging
stakeholder asks: AI in real workflows, responsible cloud spending, and
authentic internal-customer stories. We also have one credible,
contested theme (developer experience) and one structural debate (3
deep tracks vs. 6 narrower ones).

## Option A: Three Deep Tracks — Outcome-Led

**Shape:** Three full-day tracks: *AI in Real Work*, *Cloud as
Discipline*, *Customers on Stage*. ~8 sessions per track.

**Why:** Maximum coherence. Attendees pick a track and live in it.
Aligned with Jane's instinct and the offsite's "would a customer leave
with something useful?" rule.

**Tradeoff:** No room for Developer Experience or Security as top-line
directions. Engineering leadership will object on record.

## Option B: Six Tracks — Honor the Submissions

**Shape:** Six narrower tracks aligned to the canonical themes plus a
sixth cross-cut on data. ~5 sessions per track.

**Why:** Maps cleanly onto the submission base. Marcus's operational
preference. Lets DX and Security earn their own spaces.

**Tradeoff:** Attendees can't easily follow a single thread. More
context-switching. Risks the "buffet" feeling Marcus himself flagged.

## Option C: Four Tracks — Convergence Plus Conviction

**Shape:** Four tracks: AI, Cloud, Customers, plus *Developer
Experience as a Strategic Investment* framed around outcomes. Security
folds into a half-day cross-cut on Day 2. ~6 sessions per track.

**Why:** Splits the difference between A and B with intent. Includes
DX on the terms Jane agreed to in the 1:1 ("about outcomes — not about
tools"). Cuts external-brand-visibility from the program shape and
handles it via post-event recording distribution.

**Tradeoff:** Security becomes a smaller voice than the submission
base would justify. If DX framing fails on the day, the rescue is to
fall back to the canonical session list — not rebuild the track.

## Recommendation

**Option C.** It honors all three convergent stakeholder asks, takes
the contested DX theme seriously on the terms Jane already accepted,
and resists the external-brand pull that would otherwise warp the
program shape. Before committing, we want leadership to confirm two
things: (1) they accept that Security gets cross-cutting treatment
rather than its own track, and (2) they're comfortable that brand
outcomes follow from a genuinely useful Summit rather than be
engineered into the agenda. If either answer is no, we re-open the
conversation; we don't quietly pivot.
